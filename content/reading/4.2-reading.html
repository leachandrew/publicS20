Wednesday, April 8, 2020

Readings:

  • Kuran (1997), Private Truths, Public Lies: The Social Consequences of Preference Falsification, Ch. 1

Today we read about a very useful idea to explain politics, revolutions, surprises, and public opinion: preference falsification.

Tips and Questions to Read For:

  • What is “preference falsification?” How is it different from lying or from self-censorship?

  • What are the differences between “intrinsic utility,” “reputational utility,” and “expressive utility”?

  • In what ways do you falsify your preferences in public?

  • What are some examples of preference falsification in politics?

  • How do private/personal preferences affect public opinion? How does public opinion affect private/personal preferences?
